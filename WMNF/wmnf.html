What are the White Mountains?

The White Mountains are a mountain range covering about a quarter of the state of New Hampshire and a small portion of western Maine in the United States. They are part of the northern Appalachian Mountains and the most rugged mountains in New England. The range is heavily visited due to its proximity to Boston, New York City, and Montreal.

Most of the area is public land, including the White Mountain National Forest and a number of state parks. Its most famous mountain is 6,288-foot (1,917 m) Mount Washington, which is the highest peak in the Northeastern U.S. and for 76 years held the record for fastest surface wind gust in the world (231 miles per hour (372 km/h) in 1934). Mount Washington is part of a line of summits, the Presidential Range, that are named after U.S. presidents and other prominent Americans.

The White Mountains also include the Franconia Range, Sandwich Range, Carter-Moriah Range and Kinsman Range in New Hampshire, and the Mahoosuc Range straddling the border between it and Maine. In all, there are 48 peaks within New Hampshire as well as one (Old Speck Mountain) in Maine over 4,000 feet (1,200 m), known as the four-thousand footers. The Whites are known for a system of alpine huts for hikers operated by the Appalachian Mountain Club. The Appalachian Trail crosses the area from southwest to northeast.

The Presidential Range

The Presidential Range contains the highest peaks of the White Mountains, with notable summits named after past American presidents. The weather in the Presidential Range can be highly unpredictable. Mountain Washington, one of the notable summits, is home of the highest surface winds recorded in history at 231 mph. Notable summits include:

  • Mt. Washington
  • Mt. Adams
  • Mt. Jefferson
  • Mt. Madison
  • Mt. Eisenhower
  • Mt. Pierce
  • Mt. Monroe
  • Mt. Jackson

The NH 48

There are 48 summits that rise over 4,000 ft in elevation in the White Mountains. A hiker patch from the Appalachian Mountain Club (AMC) is available to hikers that hike to all 48 summits. The ten highest peaks in the NH 48 are:

  • Mt. Washington (6,288 ft.)
  • Mt. Adams (5,774 ft.)
  • Mt. Jefferson (5,712 ft.)
  • Mt. Monroe (5,384 ft.)
  • Mt. Madison (5,367 ft.)
  • Mt. Lafayette (5,260 ft.)
  • Mt. Lincoln (5,089 ft.)
  • South Twin Mountain (4,902 ft.)
  • Carter Dome (4,832 ft.)
  • Mt. Moosilauke (4,802 ft.)
